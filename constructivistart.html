Constructivist Art

Constructivist art emerged in the early 20th century as a movement that emphasized the use of geometric shapes, abstraction, and a focus on the materials and techniques of art-making.

Rejecting traditional forms, it sought to create visual compositions that embodied the principles of industrial design and modern technology, often aiming to integrate art with functional objects.

Constructivism aimed to convey a sense of dynamic movement and harmonious interaction between art, society, and the environment.
